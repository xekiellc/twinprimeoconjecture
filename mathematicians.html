At a Glance

The arc of progress

~300 BC

Euclid — Infinitely many primes

The foundation. Proof by contradiction. Still the template.

1849

de Polignac — The conjecture stated

No proof. Just pattern recognition and a question that would outlast him by centuries.

1919

Brun — Convergent reciprocals

B₂ ≈ 1.9021… The first hard analytic result about twin primes. Consistent with both finite and infinite pairs.

1923

Hardy & Littlewood — Density prediction

π₂(N) ≈ 2C₂ · N/(ln N)². Verified to extraordinary precision. Still unproven.

1973

Chen — Prime or semiprime

Infinitely many p where p+2 is prime or semiprime. One step away. Still one step away.

2013

Zhang — Bounded gap of 70,000,000

The logjam breaks. First finite bound ever proven. The world changes.

2013–2014

Maynard + Polymath — Bounded gap of 246

From 70 million to 246 in under a year. Target: 2.

2022

Maynard — Fields Medal

Mathematics' highest honor. The prime gap work is cited.

2026

Open

The gap is 246. The target is 2. The proof does not exist.
